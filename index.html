Identifying Authorship in SCOTUS Opinions 1994-2023

Project Description

Welcome to SCOTUS_AID - your gateway to unveiling the mystery behind Supreme Court of the United States (SCOTUS) unsigned opinions. Our cutting-edge platform utilizes advanced artificial intelligence algorithms to unmask the authorship of these enigmatic legal documents. Dive into the world of legal analytics as SCOTUS_AID employs state-of-the-art natural language processing techniques to analyze linguistic patterns, writing style, and legal nuances. Discover insights into the minds behind the influential unsigned opinions that shape the nation's legal landscape. Our AI-driven approach sifts through vast amounts of data, comparing and contrasting writing styles to pinpoint potential authors with remarkable accuracy. Whether you're a legal scholar, researcher, or simply curious about the inner workings of the Supreme Court, SCOTUS_AID offers a unique and invaluable tool for unraveling the authorship mysteries within SCOTUS unsigned opinions. Stay ahead of the curve with SCOTUS_AID and explore the hidden narratives within these significant legal texts. Uncover the voices behind the words and gain a deeper understanding of the legal perspectives that shape pivotal decisions in the highest court of the land. Welcome to a new era of legal analysis, where artificial intelligence brings transparency to the otherwise anonymous world of SCOTUS unsigned opinions.

Analysis Insights

One can refine these observations based on the prediction scores obtained in each case. As explained in the Methods, these scores do not correspond to probabilities but can be calibrated based on the cross-validation results. In particular, when we trained the algorithm we noticed that if the top prediction score is greater than 40% (50%, 60%), our accuracy in predicting the authoring justice increases to 93% (95%, 96%, respectively). Similarly, the original accuracy further improves to 95% when considering the top two predictions per opinion, rather than a single one. If the sum of the top two prediction scores exceeds 50%, the accuracy increases to 98%.
